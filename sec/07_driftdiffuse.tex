\section{DriftDiffuse: Parallel Diffusion Drafter}
\label{sec:driftdiffuse}

The breakdown in Section~\ref{sec:results} shows that, once per-block
overhead is amortised at $k{\geq}8$, the linear-in-$k$ autoregressive
drafting cost ($T_{\text{draft}}{=}k\cdot t_{\text{step}}$) becomes the
binding term in the speculative-decoding latency
$L = (T_{\text{draft}} + T_{\text{verify}})/\tau$. We propose
\emph{DriftDiffuse}, a
small bidirectional masked-diffusion drafter that produces all $k$ draft
tokens in $n_{\text{denoise}}{\ll}k$ parallel steps, breaking the
linear-in-$k$ relationship while preserving compatibility with the
Leviathan verifier.

\subsection{Architecture}
\label{sec:driftdiffuse-arch}

Figure~\ref{fig:drift-arch} shows the drafter. The model is a self-contained
$\sim$30\,M-parameter bidirectional transformer (hidden 512, 6 layers, 8
heads) with three input streams:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep0pt
  \item \textbf{Context.} The last $L_{\text{ctx}}$ accepted tokens from
        the target prefix, embedded with the standard token-embedding table.
  \item \textbf{Block.} The $k$ proposal positions. Revealed positions use
        their token embedding; masked positions use a learned
        \texttt{MASK\_EMB} sentinel (Qwen2.5 has no native \texttt{[MASK]}
        token; routing the sentinel via a boolean \texttt{mask\_flags}
        tensor avoids reserving a vocabulary slot).
  \item \textbf{Time.} A per-position diffusion step $t_i \in [0, T]$
        embedded into a time vector and added \emph{only} to masked
        positions.
\end{itemize}
Attention is fully bidirectional (no causal mask) so a single forward pass
yields logits for all $k$ block positions simultaneously. The output head
ties to the token embedding and produces logits over the target vocabulary
(\textit{V}$=152{,}064$), so the verifier consumes the drafter logits
directly without re-projection.

\subsection{Drift schedule and iterative unmasking}

The novelty of DriftDiffuse lies in its noise schedule. Standard masked
diffusion samples a single $t$ per example. We instead use a
\emph{position-conditional} schedule
\begin{equation}
  t_i \;=\; \mathrm{clip}\!\left( t \,+\, \lambda \cdot \frac{i}{k_{\max}},\; 0,\; T\right),
  \label{eq:drift}
\end{equation}
so that later block positions stay masked longer than earlier ones
(``drift'' offset). This distils the geometric $\alpha^i$ acceptance
asymmetry of speculative decoding directly into the noise process: the
drafter is trained to produce confident predictions at early positions
(which the verifier accepts almost always) while leaving later positions
unconfident (which the verifier rejects more often anyway), avoiding
wasted capacity on positions that will be re-sampled.

At inference time the block starts fully masked and is filled in
$n_{\text{denoise}}$ confidence-weighted steps (MaskGIT-style): the
top-confidence positions are frozen each step and the rest are re-predicted.
With an early-exit threshold $\tau{=}0.95$ on softmax confidence, we
typically need $n_{\text{denoise}}{=}3$ steps for $k{=}8$ and
$n_{\text{denoise}}{=}3$--$5$ for $k{=}16$, independent of $k$.

\subsection{Acceptance modes}

Figure~\ref{fig:drift-pipe} summarises the full pipeline. We support two
acceptance strategies that share the same parallel drafter:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep0pt
  \item \textbf{Block mode.} A single Bernoulli draw on
        $\prod_i p(x_i)/q(x_i)$ over the common vocabulary accepts or
        rejects the entire $k$-block. On rejection we fall back to
        token-level rejection sampling, which is provably unbiased with
        respect to the target distribution.
  \item \textbf{Token mode.} Standard Leviathan-style per-token rejection
        sampling. The pseudo-likelihood $q(x_i\,|\,x_{<i}, \text{ctx})$
        required by Leviathan's modified rejection is computed by a single
        batched drifter forward of size $k$: row $i$ reveals positions
        $[0..i)$ and we read the $i$-th column. This costs one extra
        drifter forward per block, still independent of $k$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Experimental setup}

\paragraph{Drafter checkpoint.}
We distil the drafter from the cached baseline outputs in
\texttt{results/baseline\_*.csv}: each baseline window
$(c_{<L_{\text{ctx}}}, x_{0:k})$ is a supervised target. The model is
trained with cross-entropy on the masked positions only, AdamW
($\text{lr}{=}3{\times}10^{-4}$, weight decay $0.01$), \texttt{bfloat16}
autocast, and gradient clipping at $1.0$. The smoke configuration uses
$\textit{max\_steps}{=}2{,}000$ ($\sim$10--20 minutes on an RTX 5090);
paper-quality runs use $\textit{n\_epochs}{=}3$ with no step cap.

\paragraph{Validation metrics.}
Independently of the speculative loop, we report on a held-out 5\% slice
of the distillation data: (i) per-position top-1 accuracy
$\text{acc}_i = \Pr(\arg\max q(x_i)\,{=}\,\hat{x}_i)$, which serves as an
$\alpha$-proxy; (ii) the full-block match rate
$\Pr(\forall i\in[0,k): \arg\max q(x_i)\,{=}\,\hat{x}_i)$, which lower-bounds
the block-mode acceptance probability.

\paragraph{Eval grid.}
We sweep $k\in\{8, 16\}$, $n_{\text{denoise}}\in\{3\}$, accept mode
$\in\{\text{block},\text{token}\}$, and the same two regimes
(deterministic, stochastic) used for the AR sweep. Output CSVs at
\texttt{results/drift\_drift\_n3\_\{block,token\}\_k\{8,16\}\_\{det,stoch\}.csv}
share the AR \texttt{spec\_*.csv} schema verbatim, so the metrics,
evaluation, and visualisation pipelines plug in unchanged.

\paragraph{Hardware and software.}
Identical to the AR experiments (\S\ref{sec:results}): NVIDIA RTX~5090,
Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct in \texttt{int8} (\texttt{bitsandbytes} 0.44+),
DriftDiffuser in \texttt{fp16}, single-stream serving with
\texttt{torch.inference\_mode}, fixed seeds $\{42, 123, 999\}$.

\paragraph{Hypothesis under test.}
Per-block draft latency falls from $T_{\text{draft}}{=}k\cdot
t_{\text{step}}^{\text{AR}}$ to
$T_{\text{drift}}{=}n_{\text{denoise}}\cdot t_{\text{step}}^{\text{drift}}$,
which is independent of $k$. With $n_{\text{denoise}}{=}3$,
$t_{\text{step}}^{\text{drift}}\!\approx\!2{\times}t_{\text{step}}^{\text{AR}}$
(deeper but still small drafter), and the AR draft at $k{=}8$ as the
reference, we expect the break-even point to drop from
$\bar{B}_{\text{eff}}{>}3.0$ to $\bar{B}_{\text{eff}}{>}1.75$, opening
the door to $S{>}2\times$ at higher $k$ provided the drafter sustains
$\bar{\alpha}\geq 0.40$.
